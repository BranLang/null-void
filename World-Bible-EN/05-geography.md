### Effect of Gravity on Humans
With double the gravity, humans had to adapt (both biologically and with nanodrone assistance):
- **Build:** Shorter, stockier, but **extremely muscular**.
- **Movement:** They appear heavy-footed, but possess tremendous strength.
- **Birds:** Flight is difficult, but possible thanks to the denser atmosphere.

## Climate and Environment

The planet underwent partial terraforming (Inetis and nanodrones), but most of the surface remained inhospitable.

### General Climate â€” A Frozen World
- **Nearly the entire planet is frozen.** Achilles is an ice world â€” the surface is almost completely covered in ice, snow, and permafrost.
- **The only habitable zone** is a narrow belt around the **equator**, where a combination of solar radiation, geothermal activity, and residual terraforming maintains temperatures above freezing.
- This equatorial belt is **all that Varietas civilization knows** â€” the rest of the planet is their "White Edge of the World." The three discovered continents â€” Terra, Simira, and Neuropa â€” lie within this belt.
- The belt is no tropical paradise â€” it's a harsh, cold landscape of boreal forests, tundra, and occasional warmer valleys. Jungles exist only in the equatorial core (geothermal springs + terraforming remnants).

### Seasonal Cycle
- **Winters last 5 years** â€” devastating for agriculture. Even in the equatorial belt, temperatures drop to near freezing; glaciers advance closer.
- **Varietas age measurement**: Counted in winters survived (e.g., "She's 12 winters old" = ~60 years)
- **Long summers**: Periods of abundance when nations stockpile for the next winter. The equatorial belt expands; some areas become temporarily habitable.
- **Cause of conflicts**: Poor planning of summer stockpiles leads to famine and war. During winters, the habitable belt shrinks and nations fight over space.

#### Celestial Mechanics and Maps
- **Infera (ARK-13)**: On the night sky (and sometimes during the day), a bright, unnatural "star" is visible to the naked eye. They call it **Infera**.
    - **Cultural Schism**: The sight of Infera divides the world.
        - **Nevriss and Beladiss (Religious)**: Perceive it as **"The Devil's Eye."** Superstitious villagers hide indoors and believe one must never look at it. In cities, this custom is already considered outdated and superstitious.
        - **Graw and Kito (Pragmatists)**: Preserved old knowledge (or intuition) â€” they know Infera is a **ship**. For them, it's a reminder of technology, not Spira.
- **Tomb of Giants (Ocean)**: ARK-11 and ARK-12 crashed into the ocean after losing Inetis (gravity was too strong for stable orbit without corrections). Their wrecks lie in the deep.
- **Ancient Maps (Winter/Summer)**: Preserved planetary maps show the world's state from when ships still orbited (shortly after AY 0). They depict a world that was different then (e.g., Terra didn't yet have forests).

#### Seasonal Changes (visible on ancient maps)

**In summer:**
- Ice caps recede â€” the habitable belt is at its widest.
- Graw (north) is entirely green â€” dense forests, vegetation, agriculture thrives.
- Terra is alive and fertile from north to south.
- The sea is open â€” maritime trade routes are free, islands accessible.
- Even areas far from the equator become temporarily habitable.

**In winter:**
- Ice caps expand dramatically â€” from above and below.
- **Graw is nearly swallowed by ice.** Northern forests are covered in snow and ice crust. Only the magma-heated city survives. Most of Graw's territory becomes uninhabitable â€” settlements and villages in the forests empty out; inhabitants retreat to the magma city.
- The habitable belt shrinks to a narrow strip around the equator.
- The sea partially freezes â€” maritime routes are restricted or impossible. Ice floes block the coast.
- Nevriss survives better â€” closer to the equator, milder conditions â€” but even here temperatures drop and harvests decline.
- **Ynetis (archipelago at the equator) stays green** â€” the warmest place on the planet, protected by its position. Paradoxically, the most habitable place on the planet is flooded with Wraiths and unreachable. Most Varietas believe it sank.
- Neuropa (deserts, mountains) â€” desert areas shrink, mountain regions freeze.

**This is the key to war:** Graw loses most of its territory in winter. A five-year winter literally freezes the land â€” magma heats the city but doesn't feed it. Graw starves while Nevriss near the equator can still produce grain. That's why Graw always eventually attacks the south.

### Seismic Activity â€” A Living Planet

Achilles's core is extremely active. Unlike Earth, where tectonic activity is distributed across large areas, on Achilles the core's energy concentrates in the equatorial belt â€” exactly where people live.

- **Earthquakes** are fairly frequent â€” minor tremors several times monthly, stronger ones several times yearly. Architecture has adapted: low buildings, massive foundations, flexible structures. Despite this, every strong earthquake leaves damage.
- **Tsunami** devastate coastal areas several times per decade. Enormous waves triggered by submarine earthquakes destroy ports, ships, and coastal settlements. That's why **airships are preferred over ships** â€” they're above the water, above the waves, above destruction.
- **Geysers and hot springs** are ubiquitous. Civilization uses them as energy sources â€” underground steam powers machines, heats cities, and warms water.
- **Magma chambers** lie close to the surface in many locations â€” Graw is the most prominent example (entire city heated by magma), but Nevriss also has geothermal zones.
- **Volcanic activity**: Occasional eruptions of smaller volcanoes in mountain regions. Mostly predictable (Maki can read earth vibrations), but sometimes catastrophic.

> **Consequence for civilization:** Maritime trade exists (it's cheaper than air travel), but it's considered dangerous and unreliable. Ships sink, ports are destroyed, coastal cities are regularly flooded. Most long-distance trade and travel therefore takes place by airship.

### Equatorial Belt (The Only Habitable Zone)
- **Width:** A narrow band above and below the equator â€” everything else is ice.
- **Climate:** Mild to cold. Warmer valleys near geothermal springs. In summer, reminiscent of Central European weather; in winter, Arctic.
- **Fauna:** Genetically modified animals (dangerous), adapted to cold. Large predators (wolves, bears) roam the dense forests â€” genetic mutations from the terraforming era.
- **Ynetis:** Lost city â€” legends say it sank. In reality, it stands on an archipelago at the equator beyond the sea, but is flooded with Wraiths and unreachable. No expedition has returned.
- **All kingdoms** (Graw, Nevriss, and others) are located within this belt on Terra.

### Beyond the Equator â€” The Dead Zone
- **Endless ice.** No life, no civilization.
- Temperatures far below zero year-round.
- Occasional expeditions venture into the icy wastes for rare minerals, but most never return.
- Legends speak of "cities under the ice" â€” remnants of failed colonies from the Golden Age.

### Graw Capital â€” Magma City

Graw's capital lies beneath a massive mountain range at the northern edge of the habitable belt. The city is built over a system of geothermal springs and magma chambers that heat it â€” without this warmth, it would be uninhabitable.

- **Magma heating**: The entire city is built above active magma chambers. Stone channels carry hot water and steam from underground into buildings, streets, and fortifications. The red-orange glow of magma is visible even from afar â€” the city shines in the dark winter landscape like an enormous glowing ember.
- **Eternal winter**: Relentless winter rages around the city. Dense coniferous forests cover the land but are buried in snow. Without magma heat, the city would fall during the first five-year winter.
- **Architectural contrast**: Thick stone walls, iron gates, and mining towers â€” an industrial fortress. Chimneys belch steam from sulfur springs. Streets are warm from underground heat, but the air reeks of sulfur.
- **Strategic importance**: Whoever controls the magma controls Graw. The city is nearly impregnable â€” access only through narrow passes or by air.
- **Visible on the map**: Glowing red-orange light above the city, mountain massif behind it, coniferous forests on the sides, snow and ice everywhere.

### Graw Landscape

- **Forests**: Dense, dark coniferous forests cover most of the territory. Dangerous fauna â€” mutated beasts, wolf packs the size of horses.
- **Mining**: Mines in the mountains â€” iron, rare metals, sulfur. Industrial economy powered by geothermal steam.
- **Villages**: Scattered settlements in the forests, supplied from the capital. During winters, many are abandoned.
- **Northern border**: Beyond the mountain massif begins the frozen zone. Occasional expeditions for rare minerals.

- **Dynasty of impostors (AY 1200â€“3013)**: Generals donned the armor and declared themselves "Grond II, III..." No one sees the face beneath the helmet.

### Nevriss Landscape (from the map)

Nevriss occupies the central and southern part of Terra â€” from Hangravall in the north to the seacoast in the south. It is the most fertile and most densely populated land on the planet.

- **Forests**: Dense deciduous and mixed forests cover the interior. Greener and warmer than Graw's conifers. Full of wildlife â€” wolves, bears, mutated predators from the terraforming era.
- **Rivers**: At least two major rivers flow from Hangravall south toward the sea. They form the main commercial and transport arteries â€” cargo barges and river steamers travel them.
- **Valleys and plains**: Between forests and rivers, fertile fields â€” wheat, grain, orchards. The most important agricultural areas on the planet.
- **Smaller cities and villages**: Scattered along rivers and trade routes. Visible on the map as fortified settlements with towers.
- **Coast**: Irregular, with small islands, bays, and rocky outcrops. Lighthouses on headlands shine for ships.
- **Southern coast**: Never freezes â€” warm currents from the equator keep the water liquid year-round. This enabled maritime transport even during the Long Winters and was the foundation of Nevriss's naval expansion.
- **Western coast**: Glaciers and frozen islands â€” proximity to the frozen zone. Trade routes skirt ice floes.

### History of Nevriss â€” Rise and Fall of a Naval Empire

Nevriss wasn't always just one kingdom on Terra. It was once an **empire** â€” the greatest power planet Achilles had ever seen.

#### The Golden Age of Spira (~AY 800 â€“ ~AY 1500)
After Inetis gave the Varietas the **Gift of the Mother** and the great battle with the humans (Archangels) ended, a golden age dawned in Terra's south. Nevriss was the first kingdom to fully harness Spira's potential â€” not for war, but for **building**.

- **Naval expansion**: Nevriss ships spread along Terra's southern shores. The southern coast never freezes â€” warm equatorial currents keep the sea open year-round, enabling maritime transport even during the Long Winters. Ships sailed from port to port, founding colonies and trading posts.
- **Colonization**: Nevriss ships established dozens of cities and settlements along the coast. They brought **language, culture, religion (Cult of the Mother)**, and a way of life. Many of today's nations in southern Terra have Nevriss roots.
- **Terraforming with Spira**: Colonists planted trees and **terraformed the landscape using Spira** â€” transforming inhospitable coastlines into fertile soil. The forests that cover Nevriss's interior and surrounding nations today are largely the work of this era.
- **Trade network**: An extensive network of maritime trade routes emerged â€” spices, grain, metals, Spira crystals traveled by sea between dozens of ports. Nevriss became the center of trade for the entire southern world.

#### The Great Flood (~AY 1500 â€“ ~AY 1600)
The empire fell not under the weight of war, but **under the waves**.

- **Catastrophic tsunami**: A series of massive submarine earthquakes triggered devastating waves that struck the entire southern coast of Terra. Many port cities and colonies were destroyed or completely submerged.
- **Nevriss City survived**: The city on its high cliffs withstood â€” tsunami broke against the rocks below. But the **fleet was destroyed** â€” ships in the port below the cliffs were swept away, docks and shipyards damaged.
- **Loss of colonies**: Lower-lying port cities and colonies along the coast were destroyed or completely submerged. Many vanished forever.
- **Imperial fragmentation**: Colonies lost contact with the center. Without ships and ports, trade routes were severed. Distant settlements became independent or perished. From one empire arose today's independent nations of southern Terra.
- **Loss of naval supremacy**: Nevriss lost its fleet and with it control over trade routes. The city itself survived, but the empire was gone.

#### The Airship Era (post AY 2212, arrival of Maki)
After the arrival of the **Maki** (androids from the ship Hephaestus, AY 2212), the situation changed.

- **Airships**: Maki brought the technical knowledge that enabled airship construction. These quickly became the **primary form of long-distance transport** â€” safer than ships, above the reach of tsunami and earthquakes.
- **Ships still exist**: Maritime trade didn't disappear entirely â€” ships are a **cheaper** but **riskier** alternative. Used by those who can't afford airships, or smugglers who don't want to be seen from above.
- **Imperial legacy**: Nevriss language, the Cult of the Mother, and culture survived in many nations of southern Terra â€” traces of the former empire are still everywhere.
